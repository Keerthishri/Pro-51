Chameleon

  1. Chameleons actually change their colour due to a number of different factors, including their mood, changes in light or temperature, or the humidity of their environment.
  2. Chameleons have amazing eyes. The bulging upper and lower eyelids are joined and the pupil peaks out from a pinhole sized gap.
  3. Many species of chameleon have horn-like bumps or crests on their heads.

Penguins

  • Penguins evolved to fly underwater.
  • While other birds have wings for flying, penguins have adapted flippers to help them swim in the water..
  • Large penguin populations can be found in countries such as New Zealand, Australia, Chile, Argentina and South Africa.

Camel

  • Camels can close their nostrils to keep out sand. They also have two sets of eyelashes to protect their eyes — one shorter (those set nearest to the eyes) and one longer.
  • There are two types of true camels, the one-hump Arabian variety (the dromedary) and the two-hump Asian variety (the Bactrian).
  • A thirsty camel can drink 135 litres (30 gallons) in 13 minutes.

Teddy-Bear

  • The term bear-hug was first recorded in 1846.
  • British soldiers in World War I, wore fur coats that they called Teddys. The American soldiers also wore a one piece fur overall they called a teddy. Women's nightgowns have also been nicknamed "Teddy" because they are comfortable.
  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.

Panda

  • They have great camouflage for their environment.
  • Their eyes are different to normal bears.
  • The giant panda's scientific name is Ailuropoda melanoleuca, which means “black and white cat-foot”.

Cicadas

  • Cicadas can't eat, getting nutrients from tree roots.
  • Cicadas live on all continents except Antarctica.
  • Some cicadas could explode with fungus..
